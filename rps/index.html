What I Can Do For You

The Regina Police Service website serves as a critical communication bridge between the police service and the community. In an era where police services face increased public scrutiny, the website presents a valuable opportunity to demonstrate transparency, accessibility, and community commitment.

I have identified several strategic opportunities that will enhance user experience while reinforcing the RPS's dedication to serving all community members effectively.

1. Brand Consistency & Visual Identity

Current Challenge: The website currently uses multiple fonts and varying design elements that create a fragmented user experience.

Recommendations:

  • Standardize typography to one primary font (Roboto, already designated as the primary font in website variables)
  • Remove the current Antonio font, which presents accessibility challenges due to its narrow letterforms that can blur together and create uniform word shapes
  • Implement a consistent border radius system throughout the site
  • Establish a slightly expanded color palette using monochromatic or analogous colors, to maintain the authority and integrity of the existing RPS brand while adding depth and visual texture
  • Evaluate adherence to linguistic style guides and ensure consistent terminology and spelling are used throughout (e.g. re-imagined vs reimagined)

Impact: Consistent branding reinforces professionalism and trustworthiness while improving readability for all users.

2. Accessibility & Inclusivity

Current Challenge: Several accessibility barriers can prevent some community members from effectively accessing information.

Improvements Needed:

  • Address color contrast failures to meet WCAG guidelines
  • Ensure all links are clearly identifiable with underlines or other visual indicators
  • Improve font readability for users with vision impairments, reading disabilities, and older adults
  • Conduct a comprehensive accessibility audit to ensure compliance with WCAG and ACA standards

Impact: Demonstrates RPS's commitment to serving all community members and ensures legal compliance with accessibility standards.

3. Information Architecture & Navigation

Current Challenge: Users lack clear indicators of their current location within the site.

Recommendations:

  • Add consistent page banners with clear page titles on every page
  • Implement a common footer across all pages to reduce header congestion
  • Evaluate necessity of social media icons in prime header real estate
  • Add page-specific features like dropdowns or filters where appropriate

Impact: Improved navigation reduces user frustration and helps visitors find information more efficiently.

4. Data Visualization & Transparency

Current Challenge: The vast amount of data collected by RPS could better serve the dual purpose of informing the public and demonstrating police effectiveness.

Strategic Opportunities:

  • Add summary statistics at the top of the Community Crime Map page highlighting positive community safety trends
  • Implement a variety of charts to show critical data, providing context for police resource allocation
  • Include Alternative Response counts to demonstrate RPS's commitment to diverse de-escalation tactics
  • Consider incorporating relevant StatCan police metrics for broader context

Impact: Enhanced data presentation supports budget transparency and demonstrates effective community policing approaches.

5. Content Presentation

Current Challenge: News summary truncation appears abrupt and images may not display effectively at smaller sizes.

Quick Wins:

  • Add ellipsis to news text truncation for smoother reading experience
  • Replace highly-detailed images with icons when used at small sizes, for example in the News page sidebar or in the footer
